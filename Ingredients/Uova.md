## Ingredient Information
* **Name:** Egg
* **Type:** Dairy/Protein
* **Description:** A round or oval body produced by female birds, especially chickens, as a reproductive cell and a source of nutrition for the developing embryo.
* **Substitutions:** Depending on the recipe, eggs can be substituted with:
    + Flaxseed eggs (1 tablespoon ground flaxseed + 3 tablespoons water)
    + Chia seed eggs (1 tablespoon chia seeds + 3 tablespoons water)
    + Mashed banana or applesauce (as a binding agent)
    + Silken tofu (as a binding agent)
    + Aquafaba (liquid from canned chickpeas)
* **Allergens:** Eggs are a common allergen and can cause an immune system reaction in some individuals.
* **Storage:** Eggs should be stored in the refrigerator at a temperature of 40°F (4°C) or below.
* **Shelf Life:** Eggs can be stored for 4-5 weeks in the refrigerator.

## Notes
* Eggs are a good source of protein, vitamins, and minerals, and are often used as a binder or leavening agent in recipes.
* The nutritional content of eggs can vary depending on the size and type of egg, as well as the diet and living conditions of the chicken.
* Eggs can be cooked in a variety of ways, including boiling, frying, poaching, and scrambling.
